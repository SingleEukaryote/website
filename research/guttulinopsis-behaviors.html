Investigating the life cycle of Guttulinopsis vulgaris
This project, like my others, came about serendipitously. No one had done timelapse micrography on Guttulinopsis vulgaris to see how they initiate their multicellular development. I was curious and I developed a method micrograph them on the surface of a culture while they engage in social collective behavior (mind you, they live in poop which is hard to image).



My findings left me surprised. Before a fruiting body developed. A small group of cells begain swarming to "decide" on a spot. After this "decision," they exploded. Committed cellular suicide. Afterwards, many cells came to that spot and started constructing the fruiting body. This observation hints that the life of G. vulgaris is more complex than previous thought. So here, I aim to investigate the potentially altruistic and social behaviors of G. vulgaris.
